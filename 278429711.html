Project Indago : Sprint 12 - Retrospective 27/10/2020

Created by Joanna Karlic, last modified on Nov 10, 2020

This sprint’s retro had a specific focus on our sprint ceremonies.

 Topics of discussion were:

  1. Sprint Reviews (15 min) – each team discusses what’s working well and what we could improve; can also include topics around planning for Sprint Reviews

  2. Sprint Planning (15 min) – each team discusses what’s working well and what we could improve

  3. Retrospectives, Standups & Scrum of Scrums (15 min) – each team discusses what’s working well and what we could improve

Sprint Reviews:

  • All teams found them very useful

  • Provide good knowledge sharing and visibility to everyone of what has been built by each team

  • Helps us keep an eye on standardisation between processes

  • Some work needed to prepare for them but this is useful as it helps us QA the build; prep does not take a long time

Things to improve:

  • Start switching up presenters so it’s not always the Mendix senior dev

  • For long processes that get presented through a series of Reviews it would be better to jump in partway through for the demo, otherwise there is a lot of ‘rewatching’ of functionality

  • Provide more feedback to the teams presenting

Sprint Planning:

  • Valuable process and runs smoothly thanks to the preparation of the BAs

  • Provides good chance to go through the tickets in detail and understand the process, this helps all team members be more efficient in their work, especially devs and testers

  • Teams spend 1.5-2 hrs on the activity

  • Team 2 Planning poker takes longer, but the activity is very worthwhile - it helps the devs and testers get an idea of how long a ticket will take to complete and therefore helps them plan their work in the sprint

No improvements were suggested as the teams were happy with their processes

Standups

  • Very useful meeting, helps keep the team connected and track what key things are happening

  • Some people attend multiple standups so it takes up a fair amount of time daily, however, not much can be done about this as there is value in spending this time with multiple teams

  • Often teams use the time straight after the standup to discuss blockers which is useful

Things to improve:

  • Standups should not be used to “account for our time” (i.e. no need to share what meetings people had the previous day etc) - focus should be on achievements and blockers

Retrospectives

  • Useful activity though all acknowledged that as we progress there is less to share and fewer things to improve; this is a good thing as it shows our ways of working are solidifying and working well for all
